## Claude's take

The "promoting without promoting" framing is solid and deliberate. You're not asking devs to try the bot — you're showing them a technique and asking if they think it's interesting. That's a much lower ask, and for a dev audience it's the right one: they'll engage with the meta-question and some subset will poke at the bot out of curiosity anyway.

A few things working in this post's favor:
- It references the previous post naturally ("спасибо за слоган, Andrei") — rewards people who followed along and creates continuity
- The three bullet reasons are concrete and honest, not marketing-speak
- The PDF feature mention (attributed to Alex) is a neat trick: it signals you're actually listening to comments, which is rare, and it's a soft public commitment to build it
- "Спойлер: пять" is self-deprecating in exactly the right way — it defuses any "is this even working?" skepticism before it forms

The gif attachment is a smart move. Showing the actual file on GitHub makes the concept tangible in a way prose can't. Devs will recognize what they're looking at immediately.

One risk: the post is a bit inside-baseball. People who didn't see the first post may find the reference to "Andrei's slogan" confusing. But for anyone who did, it builds real community continuity — and LinkedIn's algorithm tends to surface posts to people already engaged with your content.
